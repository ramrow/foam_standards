Both `k` and `epsilon` are stuck at zero throughout the simulation. When the k-epsilon model tries to compute the turbulent viscosity using `Cmu * k^2 / epsilon`, if either `k` or `epsilon` is zero, this leads to division by zero, causing the SIGFPE crash.

## Root Causes

1. **Initialization Issue**: The initial fields for `k` and `epsilon` are set to `uniform 0`. With these values, the k-epsilon model cannot compute turbulent viscosity, leading to immediate failure.

2. **Boundary Condition for `nut`**: The `nut` (turbulent viscosity) field uses `calculated` type with `value uniform 0`. While this may work initially, the fundamental problem is the zero initial energy in `k`.

and keep the same boundary conditions (the moving wall gets a fixedValue since it provides the energy source).

For `0/epsilon`:

with the same boundary conditions.

- `k`: `uniform (0.001 0 0)` — gives about 0.0005 m²/s² turbulent kinetic energy

Make sure the `frontAndBack` boundaries for `k` and `epsilon` remain as `empty` (which is correct for this 2D case).
