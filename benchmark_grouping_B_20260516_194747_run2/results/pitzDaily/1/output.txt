Looking at the `blockMeshDict`, the domain spans from x=-20.6 to x=206 mm (length = 226.6 mm) and y=-25.4 to y=25.4 mm (height = 50.8 mm). However, the blocks have relatively coarse resolutions:

- Block 0: `(18 30 1)` → dx ≈ 226.6/18 ≈ 12.6 mm, dy ≈ 50.8/30 ≈ 1.7 mm
- Blocks 1 & 2: `(180 30 1)` → dx ≈ 1.26 mm, dy ≈ 1.7 mm

While the second block has finer dx, the **first block has very coarse cells (12.6 mm)** in a region where the flow develops rapidly near the inlet. For a millimeter-scale channel with a 10 m/s inlet velocity and ν = 1e-5 m²/s, the expected Reynolds number is ~Re = U·L/ν = 10 × 0.001 / 1e-5 = 1000. The viscous length scale is very small, requiring fine near-wall resolution.

For the first block with dx ≈ 12.6 mm = 0.0126 m, the CFL number would be approximately:
CFL ≈ U·Δt/dx = 10 × 0.0001 / 0.0126 ≈ 0.079 — actually reasonable.

But looking more closely at the geometry, there may be **flow impingement or recirculation zones** causing instability. The real problem appears to be related to the **turbulence model initialization**.
