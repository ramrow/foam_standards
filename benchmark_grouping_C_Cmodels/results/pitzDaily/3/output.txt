## Root Cause

The `kOmegaSST` model involves computing `sqrt(k)/l` to get the turbulent frequency, and then calculating eddy viscosity as `nuEff = (nu + Cmu*k^0.5/l)/(sigmaStar*sqrt(sigmaKappa)*omega)`. When the velocity field develops sharp gradients or when the pressure solve produces extreme values, the resulting stress terms can produce NaN/Inf that propagate through the `symGaussSeidelSmoother`.

The key vulnerability is the `div((nuEff*dev2(T(grad(U)))))` term in the `fvSchemes` file. This term depends on `nuEff`, which in turn depends on `omega` and `k`. If `omega` or `k` develop extreme values early on, `nuEff` can become unphysically large/small, causing the velocity equation matrix to become singular or ill-conditioned.

## Proposed Fixes

### 1. Switch from `symGaussSeidel` to `GaussSeidel` smoother for velocity

## Root Cause

The `kOmegaSST` model computes the effective viscosity as:

```

```

When sharp velocity gradients develop near the walls (especially at the corners), the stress tensor calculation produces extreme values. Combined with the `div((nuEff*dev2(T(grad(U)))))` convection term, this makes the pressure matrix extremely ill-conditioned. The GAMG solver fails to invert it, leading to NaN/Inf values and ultimately the sigFpe crash.

The fundamental problem is that **GAMG struggles with the highly variable coefficients** introduced by the kOmegaSST model in this low-Reynolds-number, complex-mesh scenario.
