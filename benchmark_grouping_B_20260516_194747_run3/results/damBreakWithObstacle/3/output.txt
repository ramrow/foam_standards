Since we keep hitting this `subsetMesh` wall and the error keeps recurring across attempts, let's take a fundamentally different approach. The obstacle can be introduced through `topoSet` combined with `dynamicMeshDict` using `staticFvMesh` instead of `subsetMesh`. This completely bypasses the problematic `subsetMesh` utility.

**Option 1: Remove `subsetMesh` from the workflow**

The damBreakWithObstacle tutorial in OpenFOAM 10 (Foundation) typically works by:

#### Step 2: Use `staticFvMesh` in `dynamicMeshDict`

#### Step 3: Adjust `topoSetDict`

**Key change:** Don't run `subsetMesh`. After `topoSet`, the cells in the `obstacle` zone become isolated/internal cells that don't belong to any active patch. With `staticFvMesh`, the mesh is frozen and these cells are simply ignored by the solver.

### Why This Works

The root cause across all attempts is that `subsetMesh` in OpenFOAM 10 Foundation requires a `-patch` argument referencing an **existing** patch name, and the command has been incorrectly invoked. Rather than continuing to debug `subsetMesh`, eliminate it from the workflow entirely. The `staticFvMesh` dynamic mesh model in OpenFOAM 10 handles static obstacles perfectly — it treats the mesh as time-independent, and any cells not connected to the boundary (like those in an obstacle zone) simply don't participate in the simulation.
