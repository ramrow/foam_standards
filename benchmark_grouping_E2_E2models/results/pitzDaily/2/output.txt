## Error 1: Block hex is "inside-out" (blockMesh)

**Error Message:**
```

```

This means the vertex ordering in the `hex` definition does not follow the right-hand rule convention required by OpenFOAM. For a hexahedral block, the first four vertices define one face and the second four define the opposite face, with the normals pointing outward consistently.

### Diagnosis

Looking at the current vertex definitions:
- **Bottom face (back):** `(0(-206,-40,0), 1(-206,40,0), 2(206,40,0), 3(206,-40,0))`
- **Top face (front):** `(4(-206,-40,1), 5(-206,40,1), 6(206,40,1), 7(206,-40,1))`

The bottom face goes from left→right along x, then back→forward along y — this is clockwise when viewed from below (negative z), so its normal points **-z**. The top face goes left→right along x, then back→forward along y — also clockwise when viewed from above (+z), so its normal points **+z**.

Since the two face normals point in **opposite directions**, they don't form a valid closed volume. Additionally, the vertex ordering needs to follow the right-hand rule: if you curl the fingers of your right hand along the first face's winding order, your thumb should point toward the second face.

### Solution

Reverse the vertex ordering of either the bottom face or the top face (or both) so that both face normals point in the same direction (outward from the block).

For example, swap the order of vertices in the hex definition:

Change:
```
hex (0 1 2 3 4 5 6 7)
```
to:
```
hex (0 3 2 1 4 7 6 5)
```
