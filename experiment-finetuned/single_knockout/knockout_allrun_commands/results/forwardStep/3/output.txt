## Root Cause

The `rhoCentralFoam` solver uses an explicit density-based scheme. Near the sharp corner of the forward-facing step, strong shocks form, and the explicit update can produce **negative density values**. When the post-processing step computes `sqrt(rho)` to get the speed of sound, it crashes on negative values, causing the SIGFPE.

## Proposed Solutions

### 1. **Add `rhoMin` to `controlDict`** (Most critical fix)

Add the following entry to `controlDict` to prevent negative density values:

```
rhoMin          1e-05;
```

This sets a lower bound on the density field. When negative values would occur, they are clipped to this small positive value, preventing the `sqrt` crash. This is a standard and essential safeguard for `rhoCentralFoam`.
