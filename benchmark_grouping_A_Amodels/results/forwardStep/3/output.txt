### Root Cause

The issue is with the **vertex ordering** in the hex block definition. For Block 0: hex (0 1 5 4 3 7 11 8), the face (0 4 5 1) is listed as inward-pointing. In OpenFOAM's hex convention, the face normals should point outward. The ordering of vertices in each face must follow the right-hand rule so that the normal points away from the block's interior.

The face (0 4 5 1) connects vertices 0→4→5→1. For this to be an outward face, the normal computed by (4-0)×(5-4) should point away from the block. The problem is that the vertex ordering in the hex definition doesn't follow the standard OpenFOAM hex topology convention.

### Recommended Solution

Rewrite Block 0 with **corrected vertex ordering** that follows the standard hex convention. The standard convention for hex (0 1 2 3 4 5 6 7) is:

- Front face (z=-): (0 1 2 3) with normal pointing in the -z direction
- Back face (z=+): (4 5 6 7) with normal pointing in the +z direction
- The right-hand rule applies: curling fingers from 0→1→2→3 makes thumb point in -z

For Block 0: hex (0 1 5 4 3 7 11 8), remap to standard convention. The correct ordering should be:

**Block 0:** hex (0 4 5 1 3 8 11 7)

With this ordering, the face (0 4 5 1) should have its normal pointing in the -z direction (downward), which for this block means it's the bottom face and should point downward/outward.
