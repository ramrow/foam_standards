## Error 1: `log.blockMesh` — Inward-pointing faces in Block 0

**Error Message:**
```
Block hex (0 1 2 3 8 9 10 11) ... has inward-pointing faces

```

**Diagnosis:**

The issue is with the vertex ordering in the first hex block definition. The block:
```
hex (0 1 2 3 8 9 10 11)
```
has its second face `(1 2 10 9)` pointing inward. This means the vertex winding order creates a face whose normal points toward the interior of the block rather than outward.

Looking at the vertices:
- Face 0: `(0 1 2 3)` — front face at z=-0.07
- Face 1: `(8 9 10 11)` — back face at z=0.07
- Second side face: `(1 2 10 9)`

For a hex block with vertices `(v0 v1 v2 v3 v4 v5 v6 v7)`, the standard convention is:
- First four vertices form one face, last four form the opposite face.
- Vertex i connects to vertex i+4 for i=0..3.

In this case, vertex mapping should be:
- 0→(-1,-0.4,-0.07), 1→(-0.6,-0.4,-0.07), 2→(0.6,-0.4,-0.07), 3→(1,-0.4,-0.07)
- 4→(-1,0.4,-0.07), 5→(-0.6,0.4,-0.07), 6→(0.6,0.4,-0.07), 7→(1,0.4,-0.07)
- 8→(-1,-0.4,0.07), 9→(-0.6,-0.4,0.07), 10→(0.6,-0.4,0.07), 11→(1,-0.4,0.07)

Face `(1 2 10 9)` uses vertices from different z-levels (1,2 are z=-0.07; 9,10 are z=0.07). For correct winding, when looking from outside the block, vertices should go counterclockwise. The face `(1 2 10 9)` goes from vertex 1→2 (outward in +x), then 2→10 (into page +z), then 10→9 (back in -x), then 9→1 (toward viewer -z). This gives a **clockwise** winding when viewed from outside, which is inward-pointing.

**Fix:** Swap the winding of the second face by reordering the vertices. Change the block to:

```

```

This reverses the winding so that all external faces have normals pointing outward. Alternatively, you could keep the block as-is but swap the winding of **all** faces consistently — however, the simplest fix is reversing the entire vertex order.

Similarly, check the other three blocks for consistent winding. The recommended approach is to ensure that for each hex block `(a b c d e f g h)`, when viewing any external face, the vertices go counterclockwise. A reliable method is to use the right-hand rule: curl fingers from face 0→1→2→3, thumb should point toward face 4→5→6→7.
