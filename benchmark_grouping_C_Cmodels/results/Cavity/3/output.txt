Looking at the initial/boundary conditions:
- **k**: All walls have `fixedValue uniform 0` — this is wrong because the moving wall should generate turbulence.
- **epsilon**: Uses `epsilonWallFunction` with `value uniform 0` — also problematic since there's no non-zero reference scale.

For a lid-driven cavity with Re = 1×10⁵ (based on ν=1e-05 and U=1), the turbulence levels should be initialized to produce reasonable values. With `Cmu = 0.09`, we typically set:
- `k = 0.03 × (U)^2`
- `epsilon = 0.09 × (U)^3 / L`

Where L ≈ 1 (the cavity height).

The `k` field is entirely zero everywhere (including the moving wall, which is set to `uniform 0`). When the moving wall drives the flow, no turbulence develops, so `k` and `epsilon` remain zero throughout the domain. }
