## Root Cause Analysis

Looking at the `blockMeshDict` carefully, the error is clear:

```
face 3 in patch 4 does not have neighbour cell face: 4(4 9 21 16)
```

The `upperWall` patch (which is patch 4) contains the face `(4 9 21 16)`. This face is supposed to be on the boundary, but it actually has a neighboring cell — specifically, cell `(4 5 9 8)` on the other side of that face. This means the face `(4 9 21 16)` is **not** a true external boundary face; it's an internal face shared with another cell.

The problem is that vertex 4 is at `(0, 0.375, 0)` and vertex 9 is at `(0.5, 1, 0)`, but vertex 5 is at `(0.5, 0.375, 0)` and vertex 8 is at `(0, 1, 0)`. The face `(4 9 21 16)` goes from bottom-left `(0, 0.375)` to top-left `(0, 1)` — wait, that's not right. Vertex 4 is `(0, 0.375)` and vertex 9 is `(0.5, 1)`. There's no vertex at `(0, 1)` connected to vertex 4 in that face.

Actually, looking at the vertices again:
- Vertex 4: `(0, 0.375)`
- Vertex 9: `(0.5, 1)`
- Vertex 21: `(0.5, 1)` — wait, 21 is `(0.5, 1, 1)` and 9 is `(0.5, 1, 0)`

No — vertex 9 is `(0.5, 1, 0)` and vertex 21 is `(0.5, 1, 1)`. So face `(4 9 21 16)` goes from `(0, 0.375, 0)` → `(0.5, 1, 0)` → `(0.5, 1, 1)` → `(0, 1, 1)`. This face connects a low-y vertex `(0, 0.375)` directly to high-y vertices `(0.5, 1)` and `(0, 1)`. This face is **diagonal** across a quadrilateral that should be split into two triangles, but in OpenFOAM 10's `blockMesh`, all faces must be planar quadrilaterals with consistent winding.

The face `(4 9 21 16)` tries to connect four vertices that don't form a convex planar quadrilateral — vertices 4 and 16 are at y=0.375 and 1 respectively, while 9 and 21 are both at x=0.5 but y=1. This face is malformed.

## Proposed Fix

The core problem is that the block topology is geometrically invalid. The face `(4 9 21 16)` is not a valid planar quadrilateral. The vertices need to be reordered or the block decomposition needs to change.

**Fix the `blockMeshDict` block definitions:**

Change the block definitions so that all faces are proper planar quads. One approach — redefine the blocks so that the obstacle sits at a corner where fewer blocks share internal faces:

Rewrite the blocks to use a simpler 3D decomposition:

```

(
    // Block 0: x=[0,0.5], y=[0,0.375] — bottom-left
    hex (0 1 5 4 12 13 17 16) (16 12 1) simpleGrading (1 1 1)

    // Block 1: x=[0.5,1], y=[0,0.375] — bottom-right (includes obstacle area)
    hex (1 3 7 6 13 15 19 17) (12 12 1) simpleGrading (1 1 1)

    // Block 2: x=[0,0.5], y=[0.375,1] — top-left
    hex (4 5 9 8 16 17 21 20) (12 4 1) simpleGrading (1 1 1)

    // Block 3: x=[0.5,1], y=[0.375,1] — top-right
    hex (6 7 11 10 17 19 23 22) (12 4 1) simpleGrading (1 1 1)
);
```

And update the boundary faces accordingly. The key principle: **every face must be a proper planar quadrilateral** — no diagonal/non-convex faces. Each block must have 6 faces where opposite faces are parallel and all 4 vertices lie on a common plane.

Additionally, verify that every boundary face references vertices that co-planar and form a proper quad. The `upperWall` face `(4 9 21 16)` goes from `(0, 0.375)` to `(0.5, 1)` — this is not a horizontal face (z-constant) nor a vertical face (x- or y-constant plane with consistent coordinates). Vertices 4=(0,0.375), 9=(0.5,1), 21=(0.5,1), 16=(0,1) — these span a parallelogram, which is fine IF all four vertices are coplanar. They are coplanar (all at z=0 for 4,9 and z=1 for 16,21 — no, that's the problem! 4 and 9 are at z=0, 16 and 21 are at z=1 — they span a 3D quadrilateral, not a planar face).

**Each block must have:**
- Bottom face: all z=min
- Top face: all z=max
- Front face: all y=min
- Back face: all y=max
- Left face: all x=min
- Right face: all x=max

The blocks need to be axis-aligned unit cubes/hexes. Rewrite all 6 blocks so that each has one vertex at minimum x/y/z and the opposite at maximum x/y/z, with consistent z-extents per block.
