However, the `epsilonWallFunction` calculates wall function coefficients that depend on the `nut` field values at the wall. In OpenFOAM 10 (Foundation), the `kEpsilon` model's wall function computation requires `nut` to be accessible at the wall. The `epsilonWallFunction` needs the local shear stress information, which is derived from `nut`. When `nut` is forced to `0` everywhere at walls, the wall function calculation fails when it tries to use those zero values in its computation.

The real issue is that **both** `nut` **and** `epsilon` need proper wall-function-compatible boundary conditions. The `epsilonWallFunction` at walls needs non-zero `nut` values to compute the correct dissipation rate near the wall.
