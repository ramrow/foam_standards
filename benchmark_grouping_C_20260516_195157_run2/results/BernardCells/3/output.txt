This occurs in the thermodynamic calculation (`thermoI.H`), specifically when trying to compute temperature from enthalpy. The stack trace shows it's failing in `heRhoThermo::calculate()` -> `species::thermo::T()`.

This error means the solver is iterating to solve for temperature from the enthalpy field but cannot converge within 100 iterations. This typically happens when:

1. The initial conditions produce extreme temperature gradients leading to large enthalpy values that make the inverse lookup unstable, OR
2. The relaxation factors are too aggressive causing oscillations, OR
3. The PIMPLE outer correction loop is diverging.

Looking at the current setup: the hot wall is at 310 K and the cold wall at 300 K, creating a small 10 K gradient. With `deltaT = 1` and `endTime = 1000`, the simulation should reach steady-state long before 1000 seconds. The issue is likely that the relaxation factor of `1` (no under-relaxation) causes divergence.
