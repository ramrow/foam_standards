## Error 1: `log.blockMesh` — Block is "inside-out"

**Error Message:**
```
Block hex (8 9 10 11 12 13 14 15) ... is inside-out
```

This means the vertex ordering in block 1 produces a negative volume, indicating the vertices don't form a valid right-handed hexahedron.

### Root Cause

The issue is with **Block 1** (`hex (8 9 10 11 12 13 14 15)`). Looking at the vertex coordinates:

- Vertices 8–11: z = 0.1 plane forming a rectangle from x=-0.2 to x=0.2, y=-0.5 to y=0.5
- Vertices 12–15: z = 0 plane forming a rectangle from x=-0.2 to x=0.2, y=-0.5 to y=0.5

These two faces are coplanar (both lie in the same geometric positions, just different z-values), making them parallel rather than opposite faces of a proper hexahedron. The block collapses into a degenerate prism with zero thickness in the z-direction between these faces, resulting in an "inside-out" error.

Additionally, looking at the face definitions:
- Face `(8 9 10 11)` (top of block 1) uses vertices going around the z=0.1 face. For a right-handed hex, if we look at the z=0.1 face, the normal should point toward +z. But the corresponding face on the z=0 side would need to have its vertices ordered so that when viewed from above (+z), they go counterclockwise. The current ordering doesn't satisfy this.

### Solution

Redefine **Block 1**. Since vertices 8–15 represent a single rectangular region spanning z=0 to z=0.1, the correct approach is to define only **two blocks**:

1. **Block 1**: The upstream section from z=0 to z=0.1 covering x=-1 to x=-0.2 (currently vertices 0–7)
2. **Block 2**: The cylinder region from z=0 to z=0.1 covering x=-0.2 to x=0.2 (currently vertices 8–15)
3. **Block 3**: The downstream section from z=0 to z=0.1 covering x=0.2 to x=2 (currently vertices 16–23)

However, the real problem is that the vertex numbering needs to be reorganized so that each block has one face at z=0 and the opposite face at z=0.1 with consistent winding. Specifically, for Block 1 (the middle block representing the cylinder cross-section):

- Bottom face (z=0): `(8 12 13 9)` — reordered so that when viewed from below (+z), the vertices go CCW
- Top face (z=0.1): `(4 5 6 7)` — already defined correctly for block 0
- And similarly adjust the rest of the faces accordingly

Alternatively, and more cleanly, restructure the mesh to use **four blocks** (as commonly done in the standard offsetCylinder tutorial):
- Upstream vertical column (x=-1 to x=-0.2, z=0→0.1)
- Cylinder column (x=-0.2 to x=0.2, z=0→0.1)
- Downstream column (x=0.2 to x=2, z=0→0.1)
- Plus the full-length sections above/below the cylinder (but this gets complex)

A simpler fix: **swap the vertex ordering** for Block 1 so that the z=0 face comes first with reversed winding, e.g.:

Change Block 1 from:
```
hex (8 9 10 11 12 13 14 15)
```
to:
```
hex (12 13 14 11 8 15 10 9)
```

But the recommended approach is to completely redesign the mesh topology. The simplest working alternative is to collapse the three z-layered blocks into a single block per z-level, or better yet, follow the standard open-source offsetCylinder tutorial mesh layout where there are separate upstream, cylinder, and downstream regions all extending through both z-planes.
