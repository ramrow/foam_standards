The standard convention requires that when looking at the bottom face, the vertices go counterclockwise, and the top face follows the same winding so that normals point outward.

The problem is that vertices 0→1→2→3 forms a clockwise loop when viewed from below (looking in the -z direction), making the block inside-out.
