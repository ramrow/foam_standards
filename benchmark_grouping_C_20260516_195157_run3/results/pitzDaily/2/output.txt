## Error Analysis

The simulation crashes with a **floating point exception (SIGFPE)** during the `symGaussSeidelSmoother` operation on the `k` equation. This occurs because the turbulent kinetic energy (`k`) develops negative values, which leads to NaN propagation when the solver tries to compute its square root.

### Key Observations from the Log:

1. **Negative `k` values**: `bounding k, min: -32.1625` — this is catastrophic. The `k` field goes massively negative.
2. **High Courant numbers**: `max: 2.20977` — well above 1, indicating the CFL condition is violated.
3. **Excessive iterations**: `No Iterations 1000` for `k` suggests the SIMPLEC-like coupling is struggling to converge.
4. **The crash happens in `kEpsilon::correct()`**, confirming the issue is with the turbulence model correction step.

## Root Causes

1. **CFL Violation**: With `deltaT = 0.0001`, the maximum Courant number exceeds 2, causing instability. The flow is compressible in terms of CFL despite being incompressible in physics.

2. **Insufficient Pressure-Velocity Coupling**: The current `fvSolution` settings may need tighter coupling between pressure and velocity equations.

3. **Turbulence Model Sensitivity**: The `kEpsilon` model combined with large Courant numbers produces spurious oscillations leading to negative `k`.

## Recommended Solutions

### 1. Reduce the Time Step (Primary Fix)

Reduce `deltaT` significantly to satisfy the CFL condition:
- Change `deltaT` from `0.0001` to something much smaller like `0.00001` or even `0.00005`
- Alternatively, enable adaptive time stepping via `adjustTimeStep yes;` in `controlDict`

### 2. Increase PIMPLE Outer Correctors

Increase `nOuterCorrectors` from `1` to `2` or more in `fvSolution` to improve coupling stability:
```

{
    nOuterCorrectors    2;
    ...
}
```

### 3. Add Under-Relaxation in `fvSolution`

Add relaxation factors to stabilize the coupled solve:
```

        p       0.3;

    {
        U       0.7;
```

The real issue is that the **velocity relaxation is effectively 1.0** during the second corrector sweep because the residual-based relaxation kicks in after the first iteration. In PIMPLE with multiple outer correctors, the relaxation should be applied differently.
