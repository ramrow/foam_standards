## Error 1: `log.blockMesh` — Block hex (0 4 5 1 8 12 13 9) is inside-out

**Error Message:**
```
Block hex (0 4 5 1 8 12 13 9) ... is inside-out

```

**Diagnosis:**

After 9 attempts of trying different vertex orderings, the fundamental problem persists. The block `hex (0 4 5 1 8 12 13 9)` continues to fail. The error references face `4(0 4 12 8)` — the face at x=-1 connecting vertices 0, 4, 12, 8. This face runs vertically along x=-1 from z=-0.07 to z=0.07. The vertices are:
- 0: (-1, -0.4, -0.07)
- 4: (-1, 0.4, -0.07)
- 12: (-1, 0.4, 0.07)
- 8: (-1, -0.4, 0.07)

This face lies in the plane x=-1. When viewed from outside (at x=-1, looking inward toward increasing x), the winding 0→4→12→8 goes from bottom→top→top-back→bottom, which is **clockwise** — confirming the block is inside-out.

**The core issue revealed:** The vertex layout places vertices 0, 4 at x=-1 and vertices 1, 5 at x=-0.6. The face (0 4 12 8) is the entire side wall of the block at x=-1. For this face to have a counter-clockwise winding when viewed from outside, the order must go from the bottom-front to top-front to top-back to bottom-back. But in our case, vertices 0 and 4 are at the FRONT (z=-0.07) while 8 and 12 are at the BACK (z=0.07). So the correct front-to-back winding at x=-1 should be: 0(bottom-front) → 4(top-front) → 12(top-back) → 8(bottom-back). This is exactly what we have: 0→4→12→8. Yet the solver still reports inside-out.

This tells us the **right-hand rule interpretation** is opposite to what we think. The issue is that for a hex block `(a b c d e f g h)`, the face `(a b c d)` is considered the "front" and the normal should point from face `(a b c d)` toward face `(e f g h)`. For the block to be valid, this normal must point **away from the block's exterior** (i.e., outward-facing normals on all external faces).

In our case, face `(0 4 5 1)` is at z=-0.07 (the "bottom" of the block) and face `(8 12 13 9)` is at z=0.07 (the "top"). The normal from bottom to top points in +z. For the side face at x=-1 (vertices 0,4,12,8), when viewed from outside (which is at x=-1, looking in -x direction), the winding should go counter-clockwise. The sequence 0→4→12→8 goes bottom→top→top-back→bottom. At x=-1, this traces the face with the interior of the block to the **left** (when facing the direction of travel), which makes it clockwise when viewed from outside.

**The fix:** Reverse the winding of block 0 only. Change:
```
hex (0 4 5 1 8 12 13 9)
```
to:
```
hex (1 5 4 0 9 13 12 8)
```

Keep the other three blocks as they are:
- `hex (4 7 6 5 12 15 14 13)`
- `hex (0 3 7 4 8 11 15 12)`
- `hex (1 2 3 5 9 10 11 14)`

This reverses the traversal of the front face so that the normal points in -z instead of +z, making the side face at x=-1 wind counter-clockwise when viewed from outside.
