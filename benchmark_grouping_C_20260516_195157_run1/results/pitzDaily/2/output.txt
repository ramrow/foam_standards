### Root Cause Analysis

The divergence stems from **initial/boundary conditions for `k` and `epsilon` being far too large**, combined with the relatively high inlet velocity causing excessive production terms that overwhelm the initial field.

Looking at the current values:
- **`k` internalField**: `uniform 0.06` ✅ (reasonable)
- **`epsilon` internalField**: `uniform 14855` ❌ **This is wildly incorrect**

For the k-epsilon model, `epsilon` should be derived from `k` and the turbulent length scale. With `k = 0.06`, a reasonable `epsilon` would be around `0.09 * sqrt(k)/L` where `L` is a characteristic length (~0.0254 m for the y-extent). This gives roughly `~0.09 * sqrt(0.06)/0.0254 ≈ 0.9`. An `epsilon` of **14855** creates enormous production rates that drive `k` negative almost immediately.

Additionally, the **`nut`** field has `calculated` BCs on `inlet`/`outlet` which may produce invalid values during early iterations.

### 2. In `system/fvSolution`, reduce `maxIter` from `500` to `200` for the `"(U|k|epsilon|nuTilda)"` solver

### 3. In `system/fvSolution`, remove the `min` and `max` constraints from the `"(U|k|epsilon|nuTilda)"` solver

The `min 1e-05;` and `max 1e+05;` were added in Attempt 9 but are not strictly necessary since the solver already has tight tolerances. These can sometimes interfere with proper convergence checking. Remove them so the solver manages convergence naturally.
